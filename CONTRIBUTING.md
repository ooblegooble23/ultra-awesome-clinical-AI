# Contributing

Thanks for helping improve this list. The goal is a **tightly curated** resource, so quality and fit matter more than volume.

## What belongs here

A resource is a good fit if it helps someone *build or study* AI at the intersection of medication safety, surgical care, and underserved-population access. That includes datasets, open-source tools and libraries, models, benchmarks, and genuinely orienting reading or courses.

## What doesn't

- Purely closed commercial products with no research paper, API, or developer entry point.
- Broad "AI in general" resources with no clinical angle.
- Dead links, or resources behind a hard paywall with no open alternative.
- Self-promotion without substance. (Your own project is welcome *if* it's real, documented, and useful — hold it to the same bar as everything else.)

## How to submit

1. Fork this repository.
2. Add your resource to the most appropriate section, in this format:

   ```markdown
   - [Name](https://link) — One or two sentences on what it is and, more importantly, *why it's useful* here. Written in your own words.
   ```

3. Keep the list alphabetical within a subsection only if the section is already ordered that way; otherwise, group by relevance.
4. **One resource per pull request.** This keeps review easy and discussion focused.
5. Open the pull request with a short note on why the resource fits.

## Style

- Descriptions are your own words — don't paste a project's marketing copy or abstract.
- Say what makes it worth someone's time, not just what category it's in.
- Prefer the canonical/original source over a mirror or aggregator.
- If a resource requires credentialing (e.g. PhysioNet data-use agreements), note that so people know what they're getting into.

## Checks

Before submitting, confirm:

- [ ] The link works.
- [ ] The resource isn't already listed.
- [ ] The description is one or two sentences, in your own words, and explains usefulness.
- [ ] It's placed in the right section.
